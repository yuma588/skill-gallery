# Company Travel Policy

## Effective Date
January 1, 2025

## Purpose

This policy establishes guidelines and procedures for business travel expenses to ensure consistency, cost-effectiveness, and compliance with company standards.

## Travel Booking Requirements

### Advance Notice

**Domestic Travel:**
- Airfare: Book at least 14 days in advance (10% discount applies if 14+ days, 5% if 7+ days)
- Train tickets: Book at least 7 days in advance
- Accommodation: Book at least 3 days in advance (5% discount if 7+ days)

**International Travel:**
- Book at least 21 days in advance
- Requires additional approval from Department Head

### Approved Booking Channels

Employees must use one of the following booking methods:
1. **Corporate Travel Portal** (preferred) - Use company-contracted rates
2. **Approved Travel Agency** - Check HR for approved agency list
3. **Direct Booking with Approval** - Manager approval required before booking

Personal bookings without prior approval will not be reimbursed.

## Transportation Standards

### Air Travel

**Economy Class (All Employees):**
- Maximum reimbursement: CNY 2,000
- Advance booking discounts available (see Advance Notice section)
- Preferred airlines: China Southern, China Eastern, Air China

**Business Class (Eligible Roles Only):**
- Eligible positions: Director, VP, C-level executives
- Maximum reimbursement: CNY 6,000
- Requires CFO approval for flights over CNY 4,000

**Flight Selection Guidelines:**
- Choose the most economical non-stop flight
- Connecting flights only if savings exceed CNY 300
- Red-eye flights acceptable if savings exceed CNY 400

### Train Travel

**High-Speed Rail:**
- Second Class: Full reimbursement (preferred)
- First Class: Maximum CNY 400
- Business Class: Maximum CNY 800 (Directors and above)

**Regular Trains:**
- All classes fully reimbursed with valid tickets

### Ground Transportation

**Taxi/Rideshare:**
- Maximum CNY 100 per ride, CNY 300 per day
- Use only for business purposes
- Receipts required showing pickup/dropoff locations
- Avoid surge pricing when possible

**Public Transportation:**
- Subway, bus, light rail: Fully reimbursed
- Receipts required (digital receipts acceptable)

**Rental Cars:**
- Maximum CNY 500 per day including fuel
- Only with prior manager approval
- Rent economy class vehicles unless business requires larger vehicle
- Use preferred rental companies (Hertz, Avis, Enterprise)

**Personal Vehicle:**
- Reimbursement rate: CNY 1.50 per km
- Requires daily mileage log
- Parking and tolls fully reimbursed with receipts

## Accommodation Guidelines

### Hotel Classifications

**Tier 1 Cities (Beijing, Shanghai, Guangzhou, Shenzhen):**
- Maximum: CNY 600 per night
- Preferred: Business-class hotels (Marriott, Hilton, Hyatt, Sheraton)

**Tier 2 Cities (Hangzhou, Nanjing, Chengdu, Wuhan, Xiamen, Qingdao):**
- Maximum: CNY 450 per night
- Preferred: Mid-range business hotels (Courtyard, Holiday Inn, Crowne Plaza)

**Tier 3 Cities (All Others):**
- Maximum: CNY 350 per night
- Preferred: Budget-friendly business hotels (Ibis, Mercure, Holiday Inn Express)

### Booking Guidelines

- Book hotels with complimentary breakfast when possible
- Single occupancy standard (double room if sharing with another employee)
- Book directly or through corporate portal for best rates
- Avoid resort fees and premium amenities unless business-justified

### Special Accommodations

**Extended Stays (7+ days):**
- Negotiate weekly rates
- Consider serviced apartments for 14+ day stays
- Requires manager approval

**International Travel:**
- Hotel category: 4-star minimum
- Maximum: USD 150 per night (or local equivalent)
- Requires prior approval from Department Head

## Meal Allowances

### Daily Meal Allowances

**Domestic Travel:**
- Breakfast: CNY 50
- Lunch: CNY 80
- Dinner: CNY 100
- Daily Total: CNY 200

**International Travel:**
- Daily allowance doubled (CNY 400 total)
- Varies by country - check HR for country-specific rates

### Meal Guidelines

**Reimbursable Meals:**
- Meals during business travel days
- Client entertainment meals (with proper documentation)
- Meals during work hours outside normal office location
- Airport meals during travel days

**Non-Reimbursable Meals:**
- Meals provided by conference/event organizers
- Meals included in hotel room rate
- Alcohol unless part of client entertainment
- Personal dining expenses on non-travel days

### Receipt Requirements

- Original receipts required for all meals over CNY 50
- Digital receipts acceptable from approved vendors
- Receipts must show date, amount, and vendor
- For group meals, indicate number of attendees on receipt

## Miscellaneous Expenses

### Entertainment and Business Meals

**Client Entertainment:**
- Maximum CNY 200 per day
- Requires manager approval in advance
- Must list attendees and business purpose
- Alcohol limited to 2 drinks per person

**Internal Business Meals:**
- Maximum CNY 150 per person
- Requires manager approval
- Team building events require prior approval

### Communication and Internet

**Mobile Data/Internet:**
- CNY 100 per day maximum for roaming data
- Company-issued SIM cards preferred for international travel
- Hotel internet charges fully reimbursed

### Office Supplies During Travel

- Fully reimbursed up to CNY 500 per item
- Must be necessary for business purposes
- Receipts required

### Laundry and Personal Care

- Laundry: CNY 50 per day maximum
- Only for trips 3+ days
- Dry cleaning not covered unless business-justified

### Baggage Fees

- First checked bag: Up to CNY 150 per flight
- Maximum CNY 300 per trip
- Excess baggage requires manager justification

## Emergency Expenses

### Definition

Emergency expenses are unexpected costs necessary for:
- Health and safety
- Transportation disruptions
- Lost or stolen items
- Urgent business needs

### Reporting Requirements

- Notify manager within 24 hours of emergency expense
- Document the emergency situation
- Keep all receipts and supporting documentation
- Submit with standard expense report

### Approval Process

- Emergency expenses under CNY 500: Manager post-approval acceptable
- Emergency expenses CNY 500+: Contact manager before incurring expense if possible
- All emergency expenses require written explanation

## Required Documentation

### For Reimbursement Submission

**All Expenses:**
1. Original receipt or digital receipt showing:
   - Date of purchase
   - Amount in local currency
   - Vendor name
   - Itemized details

2. Travel authorization (if required by amount)
3. Business purpose documentation

**Air/Train Travel:**
- E-ticket or boarding pass
- Itinerary showing business purpose

**Accommodation:**
- Hotel folio showing room rate and paid charges
- Separated from personal expenses

**Ground Transportation:**
- Taxi/rideshare receipt with route details
- Rental agreement for rental cars
- Mileage log for personal vehicle use

### Submission Deadlines

- Submit expense reports within 30 days of travel completion
- Late submissions require written justification
- Expenses submitted after 60 days may not be reimbursed

## Approval Hierarchy

### Approval Levels

| Amount Range | Required Approval |
|-------------|------------------|
| Under CNY 1,000 | Direct Manager |
| CNY 1,000 - 5,000 | Department Head |
| CNY 5,000 - 20,000 | Finance Director |
| Over CNY 20,000 | CFO |

### Special Approval Requirements

- International travel: Department Head
- First-class/Business class airfare: CFO (if over policy limit)
- Entertainment expenses: Manager (in advance)
- Equipment purchases: Manager + Finance

## Compliance and Enforcement

### Policy Violations

Violations may result in:
- Expense denial and personal payment responsibility
- Requirement to repay previously reimbursed amounts
- Disciplinary action for repeated violations
- Termination for fraudulent submissions

### Audits

All expense reports are subject to random audits. Employees must maintain:
- Original receipts for at least 1 year
- Travel authorization records
- Supporting documentation for unusual expenses

### Fraud Prevention

Activities considered fraud include:
- Submitting personal expenses as business expenses
- Duplicate expense submissions
- Altered or falsified receipts
- Exaggerating business purpose
- Reimbursement for untraveled trips

Fraudulent submissions will result in:
- Immediate expense denial
- Required repayment of all reimbursed amounts
- Termination of employment
- Potential legal action

## Special Cases

### Conferences and Events

- Registration fees: Fully reimbursed
- Early-bird discounts encouraged
- Conference accommodations: Follow accommodation guidelines
- Conference meals not reimbursable if included in registration

### Relocation Expenses

- Covered under separate relocation policy
- Requires HR approval
- Not processed through standard travel expense process

### Job Interviews

- Travel reimbursement for candidates as specified in job offer
- Follows same guidelines as employee travel
- Recruiting department manages approval process
